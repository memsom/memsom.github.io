About Me

I'm awesome!

Well, at least, that is what the default template said.

I'm a developer with quite a long history. I started developing stuff in BASIC on a 16KiB ZX Spectrum when I was 9 years old. I was a bit of a computer collector before it was a "thing" and have owned a lot of old 8-bit micros (mostly all moved on to new homes), including various Sinclair ZX81, original ZX Spectrum 16/48, ZX Spectrum +2 (still have this), Amstrad CPC 464, Commodore 64, Atari 800XL, Oric Atmos, and Commodore PET (my Dad borrowed me one from work for a number of months.) I them used Acorn computers in School (various BBC and Archimedes models), my parents had an Archimedes A3000 at home, I owned an Amiga 500+ (still have this, though no idea if it still works) and then graduated on to the world of PC. Along the way I used a load of different PC's, none were very exciting. My obsession with Mac started with a Powermac 6100/66 I got third hand from a friend of a friend for a good price. I then owned a Mac Classic, PowerMac 9500/180MP (my BeOS machine, still have this), Powermac G3 desktop (beige and very boring, still have this), a Powerbook Wallstreet (still have this), a black Macbook Santa Rosa (sadly departed), and a Macbook Pro 2012 model (pre retina.) I also owned a BeBox 66Mhz for a while and probably a load of other computers for a short while.

My current arsenal is consists of a Surface Pro 2 that I use for PSP programming and lighter tasks (it is underpowered these days) and a dual booting Macbook Pro 2012 that boots in to a couple of flavours of MacOS and Windows 10.

I currently develop mainly in Visual Studio on a PC and Mac, with a little VS Code when that makes more sense. My mainstay language these days is C#, but I also do quite a bit of C and C++ on a regular basis. In the past I have used Delphi quite extensively and at one point was extremely proficient in Object Pascal (though it all seems like a long time ago when I look at Pascal code.)

My personal Github is full of little projects. Feel free to drop by and take a look.
